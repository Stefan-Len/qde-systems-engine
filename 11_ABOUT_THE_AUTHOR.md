# About the Author

Behind QDE-Systems is Stefan Len: as a futures trader, a builder, and someone
who tries to treat trading not only as something on a chart, but as a system.

This direction did not come from wanting to build one more loud trading product
or "secret setup" story. Exactly the opposite.

I started working on this because, from my own experience, I saw how easy it is
to get lost between strategies, indicators, explanations, rationalizations after
the fact, and emotional decisions.

After a certain point, I was not missing one more entry rule.

I was missing a more disciplined framework.

## Who Am I Not?

I am not a guru.

I am not a YouTube teacher.

I am not a signal seller.

I am not building a Dubai-style signal group, a "VIP trade room", or a place
where someone else tells people when to buy or sell.

I do not want to create the impression that the market problem can be solved
with a motivational video, a new indicator, a secret setup, or a loud results
story.

That is not what this repository is about.

If someone is looking for that direction, QDE is probably not what will be
interesting to them. But if they are interested in how trading/execution work
can be operated with more control, more auditability, and less impulse, then we
are closer to why this system was created.

## What Am I Actually Interested In?

I am interested in how a trading/execution process can be operated so we do not
only look at the result afterward, but also at the decision path.

What data did the system start from?

What context was active?

What model or signal state was visible?

Which risk gate allowed or blocked?

What happened at the execution boundary?

What remained in audit?

What can a human or AI agent later review from actual evidence?

That is the part I am truly interested in: not the flashy promise, but the
discipline of operation.

## Why Is There No Performance Marketing?

I deliberately do not build this on results you would have to believe about me.

Not because results do not matter. In a trading system, of course they matter.
But on a public presentation page, a performance story can very easily pull the
focus in the wrong direction.

If someone is interested only because they are looking for a promised win rate,
monthly return, or "proven" money-making machine, they are in the wrong place.

QDE is not built on copying someone else's result.

It is built so you can inspect what the system does in your own environment,
with your own data, your own validation, and your own broker and risk
boundaries.

That is less loud, but much cleaner.

## Why Source Code And Audit?

Because I do not like black boxes.

If a system prepares decisions, blocks, allows, calculates risk, writes audit,
or works through an execution boundary, I believe it should be visible what is
happening.

It is not enough that it "worked" or "did not work."

You should be able to review:

- what the input was;
- what the config was;
- what the runtime state was;
- what the decision path was;
- what blocked;
- what was audited;
- what was proven fact;
- what was only assumption.

That is why QDE became source-code-based, audit-first, and reviewable with an
agent. Not so the agent can drive, but so the work becomes less opaque.

## What Do I Want To Achieve With This?

Not that anyone blindly follows a system.

Not that someone thinks: because this is source code, production LIVE trading is
already ready.

And not that another person or AI agent takes over responsibility.

What I want is for someone who thinks seriously about trading/execution systems
to see another direction:

> less hype, more control.
>
> less explanation after the fact, more evidence.
>
> less impulse, more discipline.

For me, that is what QDE is about.

Not that the market is easy.

But that our own operation can be less chaotic.
